Norway's Best Choices

From hip urban cities to glittering fjords, northern lights, and remote villages above the Arctic Circle – Norway is packed with things to see and do. So, where do you start?

A lot of things are cooking in the Norwegian capital, Oslo, a city that has been given a major facelift. Nestled in the heart of Norway, you will be amazed with a unique blend of natural wonders, cultural richness, and modern sophistication.

Located in the breathtaking landscapes of the Arctic, Tromsø is a place where magic happens. Known as the "Gateway to the deepest Arctic", it is famous for the ethereal glow of the Aurora. Don't let the chilly temperatures fool you – Tromsø is also famous for its incredibly warm and proud locals.

If you are looking for an unforgettable experience in the nature, far above the Arctic circle, then Lofoten is the place to visit! Wild Norwegian landscape with majestic mountains and picturesque fjords offers a perfect setting for an adventure trip. The land of rich Viking history, colourful fishing villages, and breathtaking sceneries is waiting for you!

Surrounded by seven hills, Bergen is known as the rainiest city in Europe with approximately 239 rainy days a year. Known as "The Gateway to the Fjords", Bergen draws itself with colorful houses and lively culture. The UNESCO-listed Bryggen, with its iconic Hanseatic buildings, will bring you to travel back in time.

Norwegian Fjords offers a perfect combination of exploring city and nature in breathtaking sceneries of Southern Norway! See the dramatic difference between Norwegian cities and small rustic fishing villages, take a boat tour through the amazing fjords and hike in spectacular landscapes. This trip is for those, who wish to experience everything Norway has to offer!

The steep cliff is rising 640 meters above the Lysefjorden offering breathtaking scenery of the fjord and mountains. The hike to the top takes between two and three hours and it's an unforgettable experience itself. Preikestolen is one of the must-sees in Norway and you should not miss it!
