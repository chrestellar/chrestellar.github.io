As a global citizen, I move from place to place due to my work. I got the chance to travel many beautiful places on Earth that now I long for it.
Below you could find top five of my favorite places on Earth!!!

  1. Photo from Timetravels

    Lofoten Island - Norway

    If you are looking for an unforgettable experience in the nature, far above the Arctic circle, then Lofoten is the place to visit!
    Wild Norwegian landscape with majestic mountains and picturesque fjords offers a perfect setting for an adventure trip. The land of rich Viking history, colourful fishing villages, and breathtaking sceneries is waiting for you!


  2. Photo from Pexel

    Menorca Island - Spain

    As the northernmost Balearic island, Menorca encourages its visitors to slow down, relax and unwind. With 216 kilometres of coastline, from the soft white sand beaches of the south to the rugged red coast of the north, the island is a fortress of calm.
    Renowned as the birthplace of mayonnaise - invented by the chef of a French general who was laying siege to the British in the port of Mahón - the isle is now gaining a reputation for sustainability and adventure.

  3. Photo from Unsplash

    Sarajevo - Bosnia and Herzegovina

    Sarajevo, capital of Bosnia and Herzegovina, is a compact city on the Miljacka River, surrounded by the Dinaric Alps.
    Its center has museums commemorating local history, including Sarajevo 1878–1918, which covers the 1914 assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand, an event that sparked World War I. Landmarks of the old quarter, Baščaršija, include the Ottoman-era Gazi Husrev-bey Mosque.

  4. Photo from Unsplash

    Split - Croatia

    Nestled along the dazzling Dalmatian coastline, Split is a mesmerizing gem that harmoniously blends ancient history with modern vibrancy. This Croatian coastal city beckons travelers with its enchanting fusion of antiquity, breathtaking landscapes, and an unmistakable Mediterranean charm.

    Split, Croatia, is a masterpiece of time and culture, where history lives and breathes alongside the pulsating rhythms of modern life.

  5. Photo from Timetravels

    Kilpisjärvi - Finnish Lapland

    The village of Kilpisjärvi is located in the northwest extremity of Finnish Lapland, bordered by Norway, Sweden, Kilpisjärvi lake and the majestic Saana fell, sacred to the Sami people.
    Here you can find the untouched nature, coldest temperatures, and highest snow levels in Finland, as well as the highest Northern light visibility! In addition to a variety of winter and nature activities, you can also make a day trip to Tromsø, Norway, and see the spectacular fjords!
